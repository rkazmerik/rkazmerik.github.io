Travelling Salesperson Problem (TSP)

Learn more

TSP describes a salesman who must visit N cities.

He/she does not care the order or direction, as long as he visits each city and finishes in the city where he started.

As we can see, TSP is commonly represented as a graph.

More specifically each edge is assigned a weight. Therefore it is a complete weighted graph.

Edge weighting could be representative of many factors:

  • Length of edge
  • Time required to travel edge
  • Cost of traveling edge


Complexity

How hard is the TSP to solve?

Learn more

TSP is classified as an NP-hard problem.

If the problem is broken into smaller pieces, the components will be at least as complex as the original one.



The resolution of the TSP would settle the P versus NP problem and earn a $1,000,000 prize from the Clay Mathematics Institute.


Solutions

How can we solve TSP?

Learn more

TSP can be solved by trying every possible solution (brute force). This is the easiest, but most expensive solution.



For N cities we have (N-1) factorial possibilities.


Other Solutions

What about nearest neighbour?

Learn more

TSP can be solved by a greedy algorithm. Start at one point and always choose the next nearest point.

As we can see... doesn't always prevent crossing paths.

Other Solutions

What about nearest heuristics?

Learn more

We can also guess a random path, and keep swapping as long as the total length decreases.

However, this doesn't always produce an optimal solution.
